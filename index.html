I am an assistant Professor in Department of Biostatistics at University of Florida. I graduated from Department of Biostatistics at University of Pittsburgh, under the supervision of George Tseng and Yong Seok Park. I received my master's degree in Physics in University of Pittsburgh in 2012 and my bachlor's degree in Physics in Harbin Institute of Technology in 2011.

Research

My research expertise lies in the intersection between statistical methodology and its applications to genomics and bioinformatics. For methodological research, I am particularly interested in genomic data integration, models and variable selection in high-dimensional data, Bayesian methods, optimization and statistical computing

For collaborative research, I have a broad interest in a wide range of genomic data, especially NGS, RNAseq and methylation data. I am very experienced in applying statistical/machine learning model on these genomic data. I am actively seeking for collaborations with other people to better understand/analysize their data.
